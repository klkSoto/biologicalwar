    Biological weapon in World Wars


    During World War I, Germany had a secret program to use biological agents in their favor. The program consisted of infecting horses and cattles owned by Allied armies to infect people. It worked so well for Germany because the agents infiltrated the USA and infected the animals prior to their shipment to support the Allied forces.

    This German program was sucessful that it caused panic to the others countries most of them in war decided to sign the 1925 Geneva Protocol in which consisted of banning the use of biological and chemical weapons in war.
